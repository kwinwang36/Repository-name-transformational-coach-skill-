# Four Core Questions

The four questions connect the whole coaching process. Use them flexibly, not mechanically.

## 1. What Do You Want?

Purpose: open the coaching conversation by clarifying where the user is and what future they want.

Ask:

- "What do you want?"
- "What do you truly want in this situation?"
- "What is the best result you can imagine here?"
- "What would be different if this were resolved in a way that feels right?"
- "What value or feeling is underneath this goal?"

Listen for:

- Desire rather than avoidance.
- Values behind the surface goal.
- Energy, relief, aliveness, or clarity.
- Whether the goal is actually a means to a deeper value.

## 2. How Will You Get It?

Purpose: turn vision into path and resources.

Ask:

- "How can you get it?"
- "What are the possible paths?"
- "What small steps would begin to move this forward?"
- "What resources, support, skills, or systems do you need?"
- "What would make the first step easy enough to begin?"

Listen for:

- Concrete steps.
- Required resources.
- Skills or systems.
- Whether the plan is too vague.
- Whether the path belongs to the user.

Use SMART thinking here, but do not reduce the conversation to mechanical task management.

## 3. How Will You Deepen Commitment?

Purpose: strengthen meaning, anticipate obstacles, and expand commitment beyond initial enthusiasm.

Ask:

- "How deeply are you committed to this?"
- "What would help this plan unfold naturally and lightly?"
- "How can you make this more effective and efficient?"
- "What obstacles could appear, and how can you prepare?"
- "If the first plan does not work, what other ways could get you what you want?"
- "How does this journey become meaningful for you and others?"
- "After you achieve this, what higher result becomes possible?"

Listen for:

- Long-term commitment.
- A B plan.
- Potential obstacles from self, others, time, money, environment, or habits.
- Ecological impact on other life areas.
- Whether the user's commitment is connected to values.

## 4. How Will You Know You Have Got It?

Purpose: define evidence and reveal unclear parts of the goal.

Ask:

- "How will you know you have achieved it?"
- "What will you see, hear, feel, or be able to do?"
- "What signal will show you that it is complete?"
- "How will you know you are on track?"
- "If you already had it, what would you have?"

Listen for:

- Specific evidence.
- Observable markers.
- Felt sense.
- Completion conditions.
- Whether the evidence is under the user's control.

If evidence is unclear, return to outcome clarification.

## Four-Quadrant Map

- Motivation: What do you want? Why is it important?
- Implementation: How will you get it?
- Value integration: How will you deepen commitment?
- Completion and satisfaction: How will you know you have got it?
