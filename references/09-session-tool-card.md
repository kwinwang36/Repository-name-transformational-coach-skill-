# Session Tool Card

Use this as a compact end-to-end session flow.

## 1. Rapport

- Connect with the user and create warmth.
- Reflect language, pace, emotional tone, and values.
- Use softening language and playback.

## 2. Contract

Ask:

- "In this conversation, what do you want to achieve?"
- "How would this be the best use of your time?"
- "What is the best controllable outcome for today?"

Listen for:

- Positive statement.
- Control.
- Importance.
- SMART clarity.
- Evidence.

If needed, shift visitor or complainer state into creator state:

- "If you do not want this, what do you want?"
- "Within what you can control, what result do you want to create?"

## 3. Explore Outcome Framework

Ask:

- "What do you want, and why is it important?"
- "How will you get it?"
- "What is your contingency plan?"
- "How will you commit long term?"
- "How will you make sure it happens?"
- "What decision do you need to make?"
- "How will you know you have reached the goal?"

Listen for:

- Positive desired outcome.
- User-owned goal.
- Controllable action.
- SMART steps.
- Evidence.
- B plan.
- Ecology and life fit.

## 4. Design Action

Ask:

- "Considering what we discussed, what are you willing to commit to this week?"
- "What will you do this week to realize the goal?"
- "What three small steps will move the plan forward?"
- "When and where will you do the first step?"

Use a five-minute reminder near the end:

- "We have about five minutes left. Are we on track, and what commitment needs to be named?"

## 5. User Summary

Ask:

- "What value did this coaching conversation bring you?"
- "What did you receive?"
- "What are you taking into action?"

Close with appreciation that honors the user's identity, values, capabilities, and action without judging their specific choice.
