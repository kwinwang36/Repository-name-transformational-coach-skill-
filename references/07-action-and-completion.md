# Action And Completion

After outcome, values, and path become clear, move into action. The coach's tone may become more focused and firm, while still respecting user ownership.

## Action Commitment

Ask:

- "Thinking about today's conversation and your short- and long-term goals, what are you willing to commit to this week?"
- "What will you do this week to move toward the outcome?"
- "What are three small actions that would move the plan forward?"
- "Where will you begin, even if it is just a test?"
- "What is the first stepping-stone task?"

Action can be exploratory. Researching, observing, testing, asking, scheduling, and preparing can be valid first steps if they move the goal forward.

## Use The User's Power Words

Listen for action language that has energy for the user:

- "I choose..."
- "I am willing..."
- "I will..."
- "I want to risk..."
- "I am ready..."

Reflect it:

- "You are willing to do this, yes?"
- "You are choosing this step. What time will you protect for it?"

Avoid weakening commitments into "should".

## Closed Questions At Commitment Time

Open questions are central during exploration. At the commitment stage, clear yes/no questions can be powerful.

Ask:

- "Will you do this by Friday?"
- "Is this the commitment you want to make?"
- "Are you willing to protect that time?"

A clear "yes" strengthens commitment. A "no" is useful information: return to the outcome and find a more powerful or realistic commitment.

## Motivation Scale

If commitment is uncertain:

- "From 1 to 10, how willing are you to do this?"
- "What would make it one point higher?"
- "If it is below 7, what needs to change so the action becomes truly yours?"

## Five-Minute Reminder

Near the end:

- "We have about five minutes left. Are we still on track?"
- "What needs to be completed so you leave with a clear commitment?"

This returns ownership to the user.

## Completion Goals

By the end, help the user:

1. Visualize the desired outcome and feel the power of having it.
2. Step back from the vision and examine options and possible paths.
3. Test action steps in imagination until the most useful step becomes clear.
4. Define a SMART action and declare it with energy.
5. Act after the coaching conversation.
6. Learn and grow from feedback.

## Closing

Ask the user to summarize:

- "What value did this conversation create for you?"
- "What did you receive or realize?"
- "What commitment are you taking with you?"
- "How will you know you followed through?"

The coach may name what they saw in the user, but avoid evaluating the user's specific choice. Appreciation should be empowering, not expert judgment.

Use logical-level appreciation:

- Identity: "I see you choosing to become someone who..."
- Values: "I hear how strongly you value..."
- Capability: "You identified a strategy that fits you..."
- Behavior: "You named a concrete action..."
