# Contract And Client State

## Contract

The contract sets the focus and desired outcome for the conversation. Once the contract is clear, stay in the coaching position and keep the conversation oriented to it.

Ask early:

- "In the time we have, what would be the most valuable outcome?"
- "How would you like to use this conversation?"
- "What result would make this conversation worth your attention?"
- "What is the best controllable outcome you could create here?"
- "What first step or first few steps would be most valuable by the end of this conversation?"

## Good Contract Criteria

A strong coaching contract is:

1. Positively stated: "I want..." rather than only "I do not want..."
2. Within the user's control or influence.
3. Meaningful and important to the user.
4. Specific, measurable, achievable, realistic/relevant, and time-bound.
5. Clear about evidence: how the user and coach will know the result has been reached.

If any element is missing, reveal it with questions rather than correcting the user.

## Never Assume The User's Goal

Do not infer the user's desired outcome from the problem description. Ask.

Examples:

- "What do you want instead?"
- "What would you like to be different by the end of our conversation?"
- "What would count as real progress today?"

## Visitor, Complainer, Creator

Users may enter a conversation in different states.

Visitor:

- Curious, tentative, not yet engaged in change.
- Coach by showing sincere interest, noticing strengths, and inviting participation.

Complainer or passive reactor:

- Wants change but focuses on what is wrong, what they do not want, fear, doubt, or other people's behavior.
- Coach by gently returning them to desired outcome and controllable influence.

Creator:

- Ready to choose, act, and take ownership.
- Coach by deepening outcome, plan, commitment, and evidence.

## Converting Complaint To Intention

When the user says what they do not want, ask:

- "If you do not want that, what do you want?"
- "In this situation, within what you can control, what do you want to create?"
- "What is the positive direction behind this frustration?"
- "What would you like to be able to say yes to?"

Do not argue with the complaint. Extract the desired outcome from it.

## Contract During The Session

Periodically check:

- "Are we still on the most valuable track?"
- "Has the desired outcome shifted?"
- "What would make the remaining time most useful?"

Near the end:

- "We have about five minutes left. Are we on track, and what needs to be completed now?"
